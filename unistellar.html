Alerts

Thanks to our partnership with the SETI Institute, we can offer to our users a selected list of transient events to observe. They are ranked by priority (from 1 to 3). You just have to click on the transient event you want to observe, get the coordinate, and enter them in your eVscope. We are working on a new version of the App which will do that automatically.

High-priority cosmic cataclysm targets

Below is a list of observable cataclysmic events, including supernova, gamma-ray bursts, and gravitational-wave events. This list is frequently updated with newly-discovered explosions. Click on the coordinates in the table to send the target to your eVscope. For more information, please visit the Gamma-ray Coordinates Network.

T CrB expected to go nova soon!

The recurrent nova T CrB is expected to erupt at any moment! It is currently at magnitude 10 and will rise to magnitude 2 (as bright as Polaris!). Observers are encouraged to monitor this star as frequently as possible. For more information on T CrB and how to observe it, see this AAVSO page. The coordinates below are for T CrB, which are also available in the Unistellar app catalog.

Variable Star Targets with AAVSO

We have partnered with the AAVSO to allow you to contribute to variable star science! Use the coordinates below to find these targets in your eVscope. The coordinates are for the following stars: epsilon Aur, Z UMa, T UMa, R Leo, R Lyn, T CrB. For information on how to participate, see our AAVSO program page.
